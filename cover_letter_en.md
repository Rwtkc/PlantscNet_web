It is our great pleasure to submit our manuscript entitled "PlantScNet: a cross-sample integrative resource and analytical platform for plant single-cell regulatory networks" to the Database Issue of Nucleic Acids Research.
Transcriptional regulation plays a central role in plant cell differentiation, organ development, stress responses, and the formation of important agronomic traits. The rapid accumulation of plant single-cell RNA-seq and single-cell ATAC-seq data provides new opportunities to investigate regulatory mechanisms at the resolution of cell types and cell states. However, existing plant single-cell resources mainly focus on expression matrices, cell annotations, marker genes, and dataset metadata. Resources that further organize these data into candidate transcription factor-target gene regulatory networks, especially across multiple samples, species, and omics layers, remain limited.
Here, we present PlantScNet (https://rnainformatics.cn/PlantScNet/), a database resource for plant single-cell regulatory networks. The major improvements of PlantScNet are threefold. First, PlantScNet is not limited to regulatory network inference in individual samples, but further integrates candidate TF-target relationships from multiple samples within the same species to build species-level candidate regulatory networks supported by cross-sample evidence. Second, we developed a strategy to infer candidate regulatory networks from scATAC-seq data by linking open chromatin regions, promoter annotations, and transcription factor binding motifs, thereby converting chromatin accessibility evidence into candidate TF-target regulatory relationships. Third, two convenient analysis tools, Neighborhood and Context Hub, have been developed in PlantScNet for candidate gene prioritization and regulatory hub identification from user-submitted gene sets. Therefore, PlantScNet organizes RNA-derived regulatory signals, ATAC-based chromatin evidence, and network analysis functions into a unified plant single-cell regulatory network resource.
The current version of PlantScNet covers scRNA-seq regulatory network resources for 30 plant species, including formally integrated cross-sample RNA networks for 10 species. It also includes scATAC-seq candidate regulatory network resources for 4 representative plant species. Through the web interface, users can browse regulatory networks by species and sample, search candidate transcription factors or target genes, download standardized network files and metadata, and use built-in tools for candidate gene prioritization and regulatory hub analysis.
Taken together, we believe that PlantScNet will provide a useful public resource for plant single-cell omics, transcriptional regulatory network analysis, comparative genomics, and crop functional genomics. Although PlantScNet is focused on plant data, its cross-sample integration strategy, RNA/ATAC-based regulatory network framework, and built-in network analysis tools should also be of interest to the broader NAR readership working on single-cell omics, regulatory genomics, and biological database resources. By extending plant single-cell resources from expression atlases to candidate regulatory networks, PlantScNet can support candidate transcription factor prioritization, regulatory module comparison, and the generation of hypotheses for downstream experimental validation.
